About Me

Riley Schnee

Riley is a Sophomore at the University of Michigan studying Computer Science in the College of Engineering. She is a peer mentor for the Women in Science and Engineering Residence Program located in Mosher Jordan Hall and is on the Professional Committee for GEECS (Girls in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science). She was a National Center for Women and Information Technology (NCWIT) Aspirations in Computer Virginia Winner in 2017 and a leader of a National Grand-Prize winning team during the 2016 Samsung Solve for Tomorrow Competition.

Her initial interest in programming evolved from her love of video games and wanting to become a video game designer. During high school, she took two introductory programming classes, became involved in starting her school's STEM Club and was a part of the LoudounCodes computer science initiative in her county. In her free time, Riley enjoys working on independent projects, spending time with family and friends, reading high fantasy and sci-fi novels, and playing video games with her dad and younger sister.

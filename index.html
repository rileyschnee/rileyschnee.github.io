About Me

Riley Schnee

Riley is a Sophomore at the University of Michigan, studying Computer Science in the College of Engineering. During the summer of 2018, she was an intern in the Facebook University for Engineers program and collaborated with two teammates to develop DayTripper, an iOS application allowing users to efficiently plan and navigate day trips. This year, Riley is a peer mentor for the Women in Science and Engineering Residence Program, located in Mosher Jordan Hall, and she is on the Professional Committee for GEECS (Girls in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science). Riley was a Virginia Affiliate Winner of the National Center for Women & Information Technology (NCWIT) Aspirations in Computing award in 2017, and co-leader of a national grand-prize winning team during the 2016 Samsung Solve for Tomorrow Competition.

Riley's initial interest in programming evolved from her love of video games and desire to become a video game developer. During high school, she took two introductory programming classes, became involved in starting her school's STEM Club and was a part of the LoudounCodes computer science initiative in her county. In her free time, Riley enjoys working on independent projects while listening to music, spending time with family and friends, reading fantasy and sci-fi novels, and playing video games with her dad and younger sister.
